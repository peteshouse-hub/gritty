\WorkEntry{ATH-WORK-0005}{Residue}{%
\begin{poem}
\begin{stanza}
Where I go, my brick goes.\\
Memory before reason.\\
Helpless days. Naked days. Unfathomable days.\\
\end{stanza}
\begin{stanza}
Eve is innocence lost.\\
The serpent vilified for exposing the truth.\\
\end{stanza}
\begin{stanza}
Your story begins with the brick.\\
We don't grow our brick. We inherit it.\\
A brick is stable. Destiny isn't.\\
I don't look for my brick. I feel it.\\
\end{stanza}
\begin{stanza}
Old men call the past comfort.\\
Hope is a synonym of abandonment.\\
Unanswered questions harden into rhetoric.\\
\end{stanza}
\begin{stanza}
Fired and fixed. A multitude of kilns.\\
You add layers. Your addendums. Nothing more.\\
The dark days are scheduled. My brick and destiny will collide.
\end{stanza}
\end{poem}
}{\VLabel{Lens:}  What makes up who you are is handed to you before cognition develops---you inherit before you choose.}{\VLabel{Link:}  Burden / Nurture / Agency.}{\VLabel{Question:}  What part of you was never authored by you?}
