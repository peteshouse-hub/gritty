\WorkEntry{ATH-WORK-0001}{Fray}{%

\begin{poem}
\begin{stanza}
Take these shoes. Any shoes. Not just any.\\
The ones I'm wearing. The ones I own.\\
Do I give them much thought? Functional,\\
Comfortable. Practical. Until they break down.\\
\end{stanza}
\begin{stanza}
My shoes are tired. They are worn. I never notice.\\
Not until they become unavoidably known.\\
Damn these shoes. Grubby. I make a note: buy new ones.\\
Yet who am I kidding?
\end{stanza}
\begin{stanza}
\textit{There is constant noise. A constant clawing. A drowning. A constant reminder.}
\end{stanza}
\begin{stanza}
``Look at my shoes, not yours.\\
You don't care about yourself---that much is obvious.\\
My shoes accentuate me. My wardrobe completes me.\\
All designer, of course.''
\end{stanza}
\end{poem}
}{\VLabel{Lens:}  Stasis is fragile; interruption reveals the load.}{\VLabel{Link:}  Object evidence / id intrusion / external verdict.}{\VLabel{Question:}  Which voice is loudest when you are already drowning?}
